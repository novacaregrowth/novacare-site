# Novacare Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> Reference document for outreach targeting, messaging, qualification, and sales conversations.

---

## Primary vertical (first 90 days, 90% of outreach)

**UAE wellness clinics, therapy practices, and mental health centers.**

Why:
- We have proof in this vertical (Alia Bahri / Al Yasmine case study)
- Higher DM volume than general medical = AI reception resonates
- Less regulatory friction than multi-specialty medical
- Owners are often the sole practitioner (faster decisions, no committee)
- Price-sensitive but value-aware (our pricing range fits)
- Overlaps with Alia's 120k+ Instagram audience

## Secondary verticals (expand after 5+ wellness clients)

In order of expansion priority:

1. **Aesthetic clinics** — dermatology, cosmetic, minor aesthetic. Higher per-client revenue, stronger DM-to-booking funnels, willing to invest in marketing.
2. **Dental clinics** — especially family/boutique practices, not chains. Mature market but poor digital presence.
3. **Physiotherapy and wellness centers** — similar buyer psychology to therapy.
4. **Functional medicine clinics** — small but growing in UAE. Premium patients, premium budgets.

Avoid for now: multi-specialty hospitals (too complex), large clinic groups (committee buying), telehealth-only platforms (not our specialty).

## Buyer profile

### Decision-maker

- **Role**: clinic owner or practice manager (owner preferred — faster decisions)
- **Age**: 30-55, mostly 35-50
- **Gender mix**: roughly even, slight female skew in wellness/aesthetic/dental
- **Nationality mix**: varied — Emirati, Levantine expats (Syrian, Lebanese, Jordanian, Palestinian), Egyptian, Indian, European. Arabic or English works.
- **Tech comfort**: moderate. Uses Instagram daily. Uses WhatsApp constantly. May have a "marketing person" but makes final decisions themselves.
- **Existing spend**: likely paying AED 500-1500/month to a "marketing agency" doing little. Or paying an in-house social media person. Or doing nothing digital at all.

### Psychological state

Most clinic owners we'll talk to feel one of three things:

1. **"I know my website is bad but I don't have time"** — they've tried to fix it, got quotes from agencies that felt generic, gave up. Our angle: we're different. Small team, obsessed with quality, transparent pricing.

2. **"I have an agency and they're useless"** — they're paying someone already but results are invisible. Our angle: we ship, then we stay. Monthly fee is real work, not just hosting.

3. **"I don't even know what's possible"** — they haven't engaged with digital at all. Their patients find them via Google Maps, word of mouth, or Instagram DMs. Our angle: here's what other clinics are already doing — the AI reception demo is killer here.

### How they buy

- **Research**: Instagram first, then Google. They'll check our website, our Instagram, our work examples before replying seriously.
- **First contact**: prefers WhatsApp. Email is secondary. Phone calls rarely, video meetings never on first touch.
- **Trust signals**: existing clients, case studies with real names, UAE-based team, clear pricing, speed of reply.
- **Red flags for them**: foreign agency with no UAE presence, vague pricing, overly corporate tone, hard-sell energy.
- **Decision speed**: typically 1-3 weeks from first contact to signed agreement. Often wants one follow-up after the first conversation to "think about it."

## Qualification checklist

Before spending real time on a prospect, they should check most of these boxes:

- [ ] Operates in UAE
- [ ] Clinic has been operating 12+ months (not a pre-launch idea)
- [ ] Has an existing patient base, not starting from zero
- [ ] Owner or authorized decision-maker is on the call
- [ ] Budget range is AED 5,000+ for setup (our minimum tier)
- [ ] Willing to commit to a monthly retainer (not one-off only)
- [ ] Realistic expectations on timeline (not "I need this in 3 days")

If fewer than 4 boxes, deprioritize or decline.

## Red flags (decline to work with)

- "Can you do it for AED 2,000?" — wrong buyer entirely
- Had 3+ agencies in the past year — likely impossible to please
- Wants "lots of revisions" before commitment — sign of scope creep ahead
- Pushes hard on price before seeing any scope detail
- Makes vague design requests ("I want it to look beautiful, like really modern")
- Asks for template-based work to save money
- Expects us to write all their clinical content without any input from them
- Multi-specialty hospital or clinic group with 10+ practitioners (out of our league for now)
- Requires extensive in-person meetings before agreement
- Mentions specific features that require custom backend development (patient portals, insurance integration, EHR) — refer out
- Asks us to do before/after imagery or make specific medical claims in ads (regulatory risk)

## Sales stages

### Stage 1: First contact
- Channel: IG DM, WhatsApp, cold email, LinkedIn, or in-person walk-in
- Goal: get a reply, start a conversation
- Time investment: 5 minutes per prospect

### Stage 2: Discovery conversation
- Channel: WhatsApp or Zoom, 20-30 min
- Goal: understand their current state, problems, goals. Qualify them.
- Time investment: 30-45 min per prospect (including prep and follow-up notes)

### Stage 3: Scoping
- Channel: WhatsApp, sometimes a follow-up call
- Goal: confirm scope, timeline, tier. Send a simple scope document (1-2 pages).
- Time investment: 1-2 hours per prospect

### Stage 4: Close
- Channel: WhatsApp or email
- Goal: sign agreement, receive 50% setup fee
- Time investment: 30 min

### Stage 5: Kickoff
- Channel: video call, 60 min
- Goal: content gathering, brand direction, calendar alignment

Average from first contact to kickoff: 2-4 weeks for qualified prospects.

## Channels where they are

### Primary (where to reach them)
- **Instagram** — they're on it daily, often from their clinic account
- **WhatsApp** — primary communication channel in UAE
- **Google Maps / Google Business** — how patients find them; check if claimed and active

### Secondary
- **LinkedIn** — managers, not owners. Useful for partnerships.
- **Zomato/Doctoruna/Okadoc/Cliniify** — context, not acquisition

### Mostly absent
- **TikTok** — most UAE clinic owners don't use it for business
- **Twitter/X** — negligible presence
- **Facebook** — Arabic content sometimes, but owners rarely active

## Lifetime value model (internal)

- Average client Tier: starts at Tier 1, upgrades to Tier 2 within 6-12 months
- Average retention: targeting 18-24 months before churn
- Average LTV (first 12 months): AED 6,500 setup + (12 × AED 850) = AED 16,700
- Average LTV (upgraded to Tier 2 at month 6): AED 6,500 + (6 × 850) + (6 × 1,800) = AED 22,400
- Our target: 10 active retainers = AED 12,500-25,000 MRR by month 12

If we hit that, the agency is real.
